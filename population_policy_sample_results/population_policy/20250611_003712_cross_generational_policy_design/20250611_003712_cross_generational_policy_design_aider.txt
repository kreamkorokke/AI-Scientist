\subsection{Problem Setting}
\label{subsec:problem}

% Formal problem definition
Let $\mathbf{p}_t \in \mathbb{R}^n$ represent the population vector at time $t$, where $n=21$ age groups span five-year intervals from 0-4 to 100+ years. The population dynamics are governed by the Leslie matrix $\mathbf{L}_t \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}$:

\begin{equation}
\mathbf{p}_{t+1} = \mathbf{L}_t\mathbf{p}_t + \mathbf{m}_t \odot \mathbf{p}_t
\end{equation}

where $\mathbf{m}_t \in \mathbb{R}^n$ represents age-specific migration rates and $\odot$ denotes element-wise multiplication.

% Policy vector definition
The policy intervention vector $\boldsymbol{\theta} \in [0,1]^{10}$ modifies demographic rates through a non-linear function $f$:

\begin{equation}
\mathbf{L}_t = f(\mathbf{L}_{\text{base}}, \boldsymbol{\theta})
\end{equation}

where $\mathbf{L}_{\text{base}}$ represents baseline demographic rates and $f$ captures policy effects on fertility, mortality, and migration patterns.

% Optimization objective
Our objective is to find optimal policy parameters $\boldsymbol{\theta}^*$ that minimize population decline while maintaining sustainable age structure:

\begin{equation}
\boldsymbol{\theta}^* = \argmin_{\boldsymbol{\theta}} \left\{ \alpha\Delta P(\boldsymbol{\theta}) + \beta R_a(\boldsymbol{\theta}) + \gamma R_d(\boldsymbol{\theta}) \right\}
\end{equation}

where $\Delta P$ represents population decline, $R_a$ is the aging ratio, $R_d$ is the dependency ratio, and $\{\alpha, \beta, \gamma\}$ are weighting parameters.

% Key assumptions
Our framework makes three key assumptions:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Policy effects are continuous and differentiable with respect to intervention intensity
    \item Cross-generational interactions can be parameterized through the Leslie matrix structure
    \item Demographic responses to policy interventions exhibit consistent time-lag patterns
\end{itemize}

This formulation allows us to systematically evaluate different policy architectures while accounting for complex intergenerational dynamics and uncertainty in demographic projections.
